## Earned Leave (EL)

### Policy Reference: 6.18

**Accrual Rate:** 2 days per month (24 days per year)

**Maximum Accumulation:** 120 days

**Eligibility:** All permanent employees

**Notice Period:**
- Minimum 7 days advance notice
- Exceptions for emergencies with proper justification

**Carry Forward:**
- Unlimited accumulation up to 120 days
- Balance carries forward year to year
- No expiration

**Splitting:**
- Can be split into multiple periods
- Minimum 3 consecutive days per request
- Maximum flexibility for work-life balance

**Encashment:**
- Eligible after 5 years of service
- Maximum 30 days per year
- At basic salary rate
- Cannot encash if balance < 15 days

**Conversion:**
- If balance exceeds 60 days, excess converts to Special Leave
- Automatic conversion at year-end
- Special Leave usable for medical or rest outside Bangladesh

**Restrictions:**
- Cannot combine with Casual Leave for same period
- Cannot be taken during probation period first 6 months
- Subject to work requirements and approval

## Casual Leave (CL)

### Policy Reference: 6.19

**Annual Allowance:** 14 days per calendar year

**Accrual:** Full amount credited on January 1st

**Eligibility:** All employees (permanent and contract)

**Notice Period:**
- Minimum 3 days advance notice preferred
- Same-day approval possible for emergencies

**Characteristics:**
- Non-accumulative (resets annually)
- Cannot be carried forward
- No encashment allowed
- Lapses if unused by December 31st

**Usage Guidelines:**
- Ideal for personal errands, family matters
- Can be combined with weekends/holidays
- Flexible splitting (even single days)
- No medical certificate required

**Restrictions:**
- Cannot exceed 14 days in a year
- Cannot be prefixed/suffixed with other leave types without approval
- Not available during peak business periods (with approval)

## Medical Leave (ML)

### Policy Reference: 6.20

**Annual Allowance:** 14 days per calendar year

**Accrual:** Full amount credited on January 1st

**Eligibility:** All employees

**Medical Certificate Requirements:**

**Days 1-3:**
- No certificate required
- Declaration sufficient
- Must notify supervisor same day

**Days 4-7:**
- Certificate from any registered medical practitioner
- Must specify illness and recommended rest period
- Submit within 2 days of return

**Days 8+:**
- Certificate from MBBS doctor mandatory
- Detailed diagnosis required
- Hospital admission records if applicable

**Fitness Certificate:**
- **Required for ML > 7 consecutive days**
- Must be submitted before returning to work
- From same or equivalent medical practitioner
- Declares employee fit to resume duties
- Cannot take new medical leave until fitness approved

**Extended Medical Leave:**
- Beyond 14 days requires HR Head approval
- Medical board evaluation may be required
- May be granted from Earned Leave balance
- Serious illness: Up to 180 days total

**Restrictions:**
- Cannot be carried forward
- No encashment
- Suspicious patterns may require medical board verification
- False certificates result in disciplinary action

## Maternity Leave

### Policy Reference: 6.23

**Duration:** 16 weeks (112 days)

**Breakdown:**
- 8 weeks before expected delivery
- 8 weeks after delivery

**Eligibility:**
- Female employees (permanent or contract)
- First two pregnancies (subsequent require CEO approval)
- Minimum 6 months service

**Required Documents:**
1. Medical certificate confirming pregnancy
2. Expected delivery date certificate
3. Proof of delivery (after childbirth)
4. Birth certificate of child

**Salary:**
- Full pay throughout
- All benefits continue
- No impact on annual increments

**Extension:**
- Additional 8 weeks may be granted
- Medical certification of complications required
- HR Head approval needed
- CEO approval for extended cases

**Miscarriage/Stillbirth:**
- 8 weeks leave granted
- Medical certificate required
- Full confidentiality maintained

**Restrictions:**
- Cannot be combined with other leave
- Cannot be encashed
- Must be taken around delivery period
- Position reserved during leave

## Paternity Leave

### Policy Reference: 6.24

**Duration:** 10 working days

**Eligibility:**
- Male employees (permanent or contract)
- Within 6 months of child's birth
- All parities (not limited to first child)

**Required Documents:**
- Birth certificate of child
- Marriage certificate (if requested)

**Timing:**
- Can be taken any time within 6 months of birth
- Can be split into 2 periods if needed
- Continuous preferred

**Salary:**
- Full pay
- All benefits continue

**Characteristics:**
- Non-accumulative
- No carryforward
- No encashment
- One-time benefit per childbirth

## Study Leave

### Policy Reference: 6.25

**Purpose:** Approved courses/training relevant to CDBL operations

**Maximum Duration:**
- Up to 24 months for advanced degrees
- Up to 12 months for professional certifications
- Extensions require CEO approval

**Eligibility:**
- Minimum 5 years continuous service
- Good performance record
- Relevant course to job function
- Must serve 2x study period after return

**Required Documents:**
1. **Admission Letter:** Proof of course enrollment
2. **Course Details:**
   - Course name and institution
   - Duration and schedule
   - Expected completion date
3. **Loan/Scholarship Agreement:** If course is loan-financed
4. **Fee Receipt:** Proof of payment (optional)
5. **Service Bond:** Agreement to serve 2x study period

**Salary:**
- Full pay for first 12 months
- Half pay for months 13-24
- No pay beyond 24 months

**Conditions:**
- Must pass examinations
- Quarterly progress reports required
- If fail/discontinue: Full refund of salary
- If resign before bond period: Refund + interest

**Approval Process:**
1. Employee applies with all documents
2. Department Head recommendation
3. HR Admin verification
4. HR Head evaluation
5. CEO approval (if > 12 months)

**Service Bond Calculation:**
- Study period: 12 months → Serve: 24 months
- Early resignation → Refund: Salary received + 10% interest
- Retirement/termination without cause → Bond waived

**Retirement Check:**
- Must serve full bond period before retirement
- If retirement date < bond completion date → Not eligible
- CEO may grant exception

## Special Disability Leave

### Policy Reference: 6.22

**Purpose:** Work-related injuries or disabling incidents

**Duration:** Up to 360 days

**Pay Calculation:**
- **Days 1-90:** Full pay
- **Days 91-180:** Half pay
- **Days 181-360:** Unpaid (position reserved)

**Eligibility:**
- Injury occurred during work or work-related activity
- Medical board certification required
- Incident must be reported within 24 hours

**Required Documents:**
1. **Incident Report:** Detailed description of incident
2. **Incident Date:** When disabling event occurred
3. **Medical Certificate:** From hospital/MBBS doctor
4. **Medical Board Report:** For extended cases
5. **Witness Statements:** If applicable

**Incident Date Validation:**
- Must be within 3 months of leave start date
- Later incidents require fresh application
- Multiple incidents tracked separately

**Pay Calculation Example:**
```
Total leave: 200 days
- Days 1-90: Full pay (90 days)
- Days 91-180: Half pay (90 days)
- Days 181-200: Unpaid (20 days)
```

**Medical Board:**
- Required for leave > 90 days
- Evaluates disability and recovery prognosis
- Recommends treatment and duration
- Re-evaluation at 90-day intervals

**Return to Work:**
- Fitness certificate mandatory
- May require rehabilitation program
- Light duty assignment possible
- Position guaranteed

**Compensation:**
- Separate from leave policy
- Handled by insurance/HR
- Medical expenses covered
- Disability benefits if applicable

## Quarantine Leave

### Policy Reference: 6.26

**Purpose:** Mandatory government quarantine or COVID-19 isolation

**Duration:** As per government guidelines (typically 14-21 days)

**Eligibility:** All employees subject to quarantine order

**Required Documents:**
1. Government quarantine order
2. COVID-19 test results (if applicable)
3. Medical certificate
4. Health authority clearance for return

**Salary:** Full pay

**Characteristics:**
- Does not deduct from any leave balance
- Automatic approval upon submission of documents
- No advance notice required (emergency basis)
- Position fully protected

**Return to Work:**
- Negative test result required
- Health authority clearance
- May work from home during quarantine if possible

## Special Leave

### Policy Reference: 6.19.c

**Source:** Conversion from Earned Leave excess over 60 days

**Calculation:**
```
If EL balance > 60 days:
  Special Leave = (EL balance - 60) / 2

Example:
  EL balance: 75 days
  Excess: 75 - 60 = 15 days
  Special Leave granted: 15 / 2 = 7.5 days
```

**Usage:**
- Medical purposes (beyond annual ML quota)
- Rest/recuperation outside Bangladesh
- Cannot be used for casual purposes
- Requires justification and approval

**Approval:**
- HR Admin: Up to 7 days
- HR Head: 8-15 days
- CEO: Above 15 days

**Characteristics:**
- Separate balance from EL
- Can accumulate
- No encashment
- Carries forward

## Extraordinary Leave (Without Pay)

### Policy Reference: 6.21

**Purpose:** Personal reasons when paid leave exhausted

**Maximum Duration:**
- 90 days per year
- Up to 180 days with CEO approval

**Eligibility:**
- All employees
- Must have utilized all paid leave
- Valid personal reason required

**Prerequisites:**
1. **All paid leave must be exhausted:**
   - Casual Leave: 0 days remaining
   - Medical Leave: 0 days remaining (for medical reasons)
   - Earned Leave: 0 days remaining

2. **System validation:**
   - Automatically checks balance before approval
   - Cannot apply if paid leave available
   - Error shown if prerequisites not met

**Conditions:**
- No salary during extraordinary leave
- Benefits suspended
- Service continuity maintained
- Annual increment eligibility affected
- Position not guaranteed beyond 90 days

**Approval Process:**
1. Employee submits request + justification
2. HR Admin verifies leave balances
3. HR Head reviews and approves (up to 90 days)
4. CEO approval required (91-180 days)

## Encashment

### Policy Reference: 6.18.d

**Eligibility:**
- Minimum 5 years of service
- Earned Leave balance ≥ 15 days
- Maximum 30 days per year

**Calculation:**
- Rate: Basic salary per day
- Formula: (Basic Salary / 30) × Days encashed

**Restrictions:**
- Cannot encash below 15 days balance
- Annual limit: 30 days
- Once per calendar year
- Cannot reverse after approval

**Process:**
1. Employee submits encashment request
2. HR verifies balance and eligibility
3. Finance processes payment
4. Balance deducted from EL

**Tax Implications:**
- Subject to income tax
- Included in annual salary statement

## Approval Workflows

### Standard Workflow

```
Employee submits
    ↓
HR Admin reviews
    ↓
Department Head approves
    ↓
HR Head final approval
    ↓
Approved
```

### Role-Based Variations

**Employee Leave:**
- HR Admin → Dept Head → Approved

**Department Head Leave:**
- HR Admin → HR Head → Approved

**HR Staff Leave:**
- HR Head → CEO → Approved

**Extended Leave (> 30 days):**
- HR Admin → Dept Head → HR Head → CEO → Approved

**Study Leave:**
- HR Admin → Dept Head → HR Head → CEO → Approved

**Special Disability:**
- HR Admin → Medical Board → HR Head → CEO → Approved

### Approval Timeframes

- HR Admin: Within 1 business day
- Department Head: Within 2 business days
- HR Head: Within 3 business days
- CEO: Within 5 business days

### Escalation

If approver doesn't respond within timeframe:
- Automatic reminder after 50% of timeframe
- Escalation to next level after 100% of timeframe
- Notification to HR Head

## Conversions

### Earned Leave to Special Leave

**Trigger:** EL balance exceeds 60 days

**Formula:** `Special Leave = (EL - 60) / 2`

**Process:**
- Automatic calculation at year-end
- Employee notification
- Balance update
- Audit log entry

**Example:**
```
EL balance on Dec 31: 75 days
Excess: 75 - 60 = 15 days
Special Leave credited: 15 / 2 = 7.5 days
New EL balance: 60 days
New Special Leave balance: 7.5 days
```

### Other Conversions (by request)

**Medical Leave from Earned Leave:**
- When ML exhausted
- Medical emergency
- HR Head approval required
- Uses EL balance

**Special Circumstances:**
- CEO may approve exceptional conversions
- Full documentation required
- Audit trail maintained

## Holiday Policies

### Public Holidays

- Declared by government
- All employees off duty
- Full pay
- If worked: 2x pay or compensatory off

### Optional Holidays

- Employee's choice
- Limited to 2-3 per year
- Advance notice required
- Subject to work requirements

### Weekend Policy

- Friday-Saturday weekend
- If worked: Compensatory off
- Approval required
